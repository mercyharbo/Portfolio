# CodeWithMercy Content Strategy

CodeWithMercy should remain a single-page developer portfolio supported by two high-value content engines:

1. Contentful-powered blog posts for evergreen search traffic.
2. Detailed project case studies for recruiter, client, and product credibility.

The current homepage already has the right direction: dark premium visual identity, direct positioning, services, project proof, experience, and contact. The next phase should improve clarity, crawlable depth, and internal linking without redesigning the site.

### Homepage Recommendations

- Keep the homepage as the main personal brand landing page.
- Preserve the current layout, dark theme, card style, motion direction, and project-focused structure.
- Strengthen the hero copy around "Frontend Engineer", "React Developer", "Next.js Developer", and "TypeScript Developer" without keyword stuffing.
- Improve the About section with clearer positioning around business outcomes, frontend architecture, and product delivery.
- Keep Services on the homepage instead of creating `/services`.
- Add a compact FAQ section near the bottom to capture long-tail questions.
- Add testimonials only if they are real and attributable.
- Link Featured Project cards to future crawlable case-study pages.
- Add a small "Read my writing" or "Latest insights" section only after the blog has real posts.

## Internal Linking Recommendations

- Homepage Hero CTA should continue to open contact.
- Homepage project cards should link to `/projects/[slug]`.
- Project dialogs can remain as quick previews, but full case-study pages should be crawlable.
- Services should link to relevant case studies once those pages exist.
- Blog posts should link to related case studies.
- Case studies should link to relevant blog posts.
- Footer should add Blog and Projects links once those routes exist.
- Blog posts should link back to the homepage contact CTA where relevant.

## Suggested Publishing Schedule

### First 30 Days

- Week 1: Publish OpenRole case study.
- Week 2: Publish first Next.js article.
- Week 3: Publish Leban Street case study.
- Week 4: Publish first performance article.

### Ongoing

- Publish 2 blog posts per month.
- Publish 1 case study or project update per month until all major projects are covered.
- Refresh one older post or case study every month.
- Review Google Search Console monthly and adjust topics based on impressions.
